Basic Terms

- Long stock: This refers to when  you own company stock. Simply put, 
when you buy shares of stock, you're long stock.
- Short Stock: Yes, it's possible to make money on stocks going down.
Shorting allows you to sell shares you don't already own (borrowing, really)
at a higher price, and at some future point, you buy back the shares at a 
hopefully lower price.

- Bull Market: A rising market. A trader who is "bullish" is speculating 
that stock prices and the market overall will fall.
- Bear Market: A falling market. A trader who is "bearish" is speculating that
stock prices and the market overall will fall.

- Options: Contracts that are listed on an exchange. When bought or sold, 
options give the investor the right or obligation to buy or sell a security 
or other asset, known as the "underlying," at a certain price. An option is a 
type of derivative investment because it derives its price in part from the 
underlying.
